'An Enemy of the People Act 2' by Henrik Ibsen

Dr. Stockmann.

(going to the door) Father!—is it you?

Mrs. Stockmann.

Ah, Mr. Kiil—good morning, good morning!

Mrs. Stockmann.

But come along in.

Morten Kiil.

If it is true, I will; if not, I am off.

Dr. Stockmann.

If what is true?

Morten Kiil.

This tale about the water supply, is it true?

Dr. Stockmann.

Certainly it is true, but how did you come to hear it?

Morten Kiil.

(coming in) Petra ran in on her way to the school—

Dr. Stockmann.

Did she?

Morten Kiil.

Yes; and she declares that—I thought she was only making a fool of me—but it isn't like Petra to do that.

Dr. Stockmann.

Of course not. How could you imagine such a thing!
